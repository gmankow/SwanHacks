Objectives

Students will understand and apply:

  • Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division with decimals
  • Alignment of place values when operating with decimals
  • Models and visual representations (grids, number lines, area models) to make sense of decimal operations
  • Strategies for estimating and checking the reasonableness of answers
  • How place-value shifts relate to multiplying or dividing decimals by powers of ten

Key Content

1. Understanding Decimal Structure

  • Introduce and review decimal place value from tenths through thousandths.
  • Emphasize that the decimal point separates whole numbers from fractional parts.
  • Connect each place to powers of ten:
    • Each place to the right is 1/10 the value.
    • Each place to the left is 10 times the value.
  • Model how decimal digits represent parts of a whole using grids, money, and base-ten visuals.

2. Adding and Subtracting Decimals

  • Teach students to line up decimal points before adding or subtracting.
  • Review that place values must match (tenths with tenths, hundredths with hundredths).
  • Model using:
    • Base-ten blocks
    • Place-value charts
    • Money representations
  • Reinforce checking work using estimation to see if the answer is reasonable.

3. Multiplying Decimals

  • Explain multiplication using models first:
    • Area models
    • Grids (100-squares)
    • Repeated addition representations
  • Show how to multiply normally (ignoring the decimal), then place the decimal in the product according to the total number of decimal places.
  • Connect this to powers of ten: the decimal shifts left when multiplying by tenths, hundredths, etc.
  • Practice multiplying decimals by whole numbers and by other decimals.

4. Dividing Decimals

  • Teach division conceptually:
    • Sharing/grids
    • Measurement models
    • Number lines
  • Explain that when dividing by a decimal, students can multiply both numbers by the same power of ten to make the divisor a whole number.
  • Model decimal shifts clearly (e.g., multiply both by 10, 100, or 1,000).
  • Use long division or partial quotients to divide decimals by whole numbers and by decimals.
  • Practice interpreting remainders or decimal digits in real-world contexts.

5. Rounding and Estimation with Decimals

  • Review rounding rules for decimals:
    • Find the rounding place.
    • Look at the digit to the right.
    • Round up or keep the digit the same.
  • Use rounding and compatible numbers to estimate solutions and check reasonableness of decimal operations.
  • Practice rounding to tenths, hundredths, and thousandths.

6. Powers of Ten and Decimal Shifts

  • Explain how multiplying or dividing decimals by 10, 100, or 1,000 moves digits left or right.
  • Model with place-value charts to show how the decimal point appears to “move.”
  • Give examples:
    • ×10 → digits shift 1 place left
    • ÷100 → digits shift 2 places right
  • Reinforce that this is a predictable pattern based on place value.
