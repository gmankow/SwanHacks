Objectives

Students will understand and apply:

  • Measuring length, weight/mass, volume, and temperature using standard units
  • Converting measurements within a system (metric and customary)
  • Reading and interpreting line plots, bar graphs, pictographs, and tables
  • Representing data using appropriate graphs and charts
  • Solving real-world problems involving measurement, time, and data displays

Key Content

1. Measurement Units and Tools

  • Introduce and review common measurement tools: rulers, meter sticks, scales, measuring cups, and thermometers.
  • Teach standard units:
    • Length: inches, feet, yards, miles; millimeters, centimeters, meters, kilometers
    • Mass/Weight: ounces, pounds, tons; grams, kilograms
    • Volume/Capacity: cups, pints, quarts, gallons; milliliters, liters
    • Temperature: Fahrenheit and Celsius
  • Model how to choose the correct tool and unit for different situations.
  • Emphasize accuracy and estimating before measuring.

2. Converting Measurements

  • Teach how to convert within the same system:
    • Customary: inches ↔ feet, feet ↔ yards, cups ↔ pints ↔ quarts ↔ gallons
    • Metric: millimeters ↔ centimeters ↔ meters ↔ kilometers
  • Show conversion using:
    • Multiplication or division
    • Conversion charts
    • Number lines
  • Reinforce that metric conversions follow powers of ten, making them predictable and pattern-based.

3. Understanding and Representing Data

  • Introduce different types of data displays:
    • Picture graphs
    • Bar graphs
    • Line plots
    • Tables
    • Frequency charts
  • Model how to collect data, organize it, and choose an appropriate graph.
  • Teach how to label axes, intervals, and units clearly.
  • Emphasize reading graphs carefully to understand totals, differences, and trends.

4. Interpreting Data

  • Teach students how to:
    • Find maximums, minimums, and mode
    • Compare categories or data points
    • Calculate totals and differences directly from graphs
    • Draw conclusions or make predictions based on displayed data
  • Use real-world scenarios to show the importance of understanding what data tells us.

5. Solving Measurement Word Problems

  • Model multi-step problems that involve:
    • Converting units
    • Using operations with measurement
    • Reading a graph to solve a problem
    • Measuring elapsed time
    • Comparing quantities across categories
  • Emphasize showing work, using diagrams when helpful, and checking for reasonableness of answers.
