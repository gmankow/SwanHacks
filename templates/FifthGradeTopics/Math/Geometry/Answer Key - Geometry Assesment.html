Part A: Lines & Angles

Drawings (student-dependent)

  • Line: Extends forever in both directions.
  • Line segment: Has two endpoints.
  • Ray: Has one endpoint and extends infinitely in one direction.

(Teacher checks based on student drawings.)

Angle Types

  • a. 32° → Acute
  • b. 90° → Right
  • c. 150° → Obtuse
  • d. 180° → Straight

Line Relationships (concept review)

  • Parallel: Lines that never meet.
  • Perpendicular: Lines that meet at 90°.
  • Intersecting: Lines that cross but are not 90°.

Part B: Classifying Shapes

Triangles

  • a. 60°, 60°, 60° → Equilateral, Acute
  • b. 110°, 40°, 30° → Obtuse, Scalene
  • c. 65°, 65°, 50° → Isosceles, Acute

Quadrilaterals

  • a. Four equal sides, no right angles → Rhombus
  • b. Opposite sides parallel, all angles 90° → Rectangle
  • c. One pair of parallel sides → Trapezoid

Part C: Area & Perimeter

Rectangle: Area = l × w = 12 × 9 = 108 m²

Triangle perimeter:
8 cm + 12 cm + 15 cm = 35 cm

Composite rectilinear shape:

  • Rectangle A: 10 × 4 → Area A = 40
  • Rectangle B: 6 × 4 → Area B = 24
  • Total Area = 40 + 24 = 64 square units

Part D: Volume

Rectangular prism:
V = 7 × 5 × 3 = 105 cm³

Box with volume 360 cm³, base 9 × 4:
360 = 9 × 4 × h → 360 = 36h → h = 10 cm

Part E: Coordinate Plane

Points: A(1, 2), B(1, 6), C(6, 6), D(6, 2)

  • Shape formed: Rectangle
  • Side lengths: vertical = 4 units, horizontal = 5 units
  • Perimeter: P = 2(4 + 5) = 18 units
  • Area: A = 5 × 4 = 20 square units

Part F: Word Problems

  • Pool area:
    15 × 8 = 120 m²
  • Triangle angle:
    Sum of angles = 180°
    180 − (50 + 65) = 180 − 115 = 65°
  • Storage bin volume:
    10 × 6 × 4 = 240 cm³

Part G: Challenge Problems

  • Squares vs. Rectangles:
    All squares are rectangles because they have 4 right angles and opposite sides parallel.
    Not all rectangles are squares because rectangles do not require 4 equal sides.
  • Shape with 4 equal sides and perpendicular diagonals:
    This is a rhombus (if the diagonals are also equal in length, it would be a square).
    A rhombus has 4 equal sides, and in this case the diagonals intersect at right angles.
