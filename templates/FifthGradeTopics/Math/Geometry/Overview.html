Objectives

Students will understand and apply:

  • Classifying two-dimensional figures based on properties (angles, sides, symmetry)
  • Understanding and identifying types of angles and lines
  • Measuring angles using a protractor
  • Calculating area and perimeter of rectangles and composite figures
  • Understanding concepts of volume for rectangular prisms
  • Recognizing and drawing geometric shapes on coordinate planes

Key Content

1. Lines, Line Segments, Rays, and Angles

  • Introduce geometry vocabulary:
    • Lines extend forever in both directions.
    • Line segments have two endpoints.
    • Rays begin at one point and extend infinitely.
    • Angles are formed by two rays with a shared endpoint (vertex).
  • Teach angle types:
    • Acute (< 90°)
    • Right (90°)
    • Obtuse (> 90° and < 180°)
    • Straight (180°)
  • Model how to identify and classify these using diagrams.
  • Explain parallel, perpendicular, and intersecting lines with visuals.

2. Classifying 2-D Figures

  • Teach how to classify shapes by their attributes:
    • Triangles by angle (acute, right, obtuse) and sides (scalene, isosceles, equilateral).
    • Quadrilaterals (parallelogram, rectangle, rhombus, square, trapezoid).
    • Polygons by number of sides (pentagon, hexagon, octagon).
  • Emphasize defining attributes, such as:
    • Number of sides.
    • Equal sides/equal angles.
    • Presence of parallel or perpendicular lines.
  • Use Venn diagrams and charts to show overlapping categories (e.g., all squares are rectangles).

3. Measuring Angles

  • Teach how to use a protractor:
    • Align the baseline.
    • Place the vertex correctly.
    • Read the correct scale (inner or outer).
  • Practice measuring and drawing angles to the nearest degree.
  • Explain that angle size measures rotation, not line length.

4. Area and Perimeter

  • Review key ideas:
    • Perimeter: distance around a shape.
    • Area of rectangles and squares: A = l × w.
    • Composite area: break shapes into smaller rectangles.
  • Demonstrate real-world connections (fences, flooring, posters, fields).
  • Emphasize distinguishing when a problem asks for area or perimeter.

5. Volume of Rectangular Prisms

  • Introduce volume as the amount of space inside a 3-D object.
  • Teach formula: V = l × w × h.
  • Use unit cubes to model how volume builds layer by layer.
  • Connect volume to real-world objects: boxes, aquariums, storage bins.

6. Coordinate Plane Basics

  • Introduce the coordinate grid:
    • x-axis (horizontal)
    • y-axis (vertical)
    • Origin (0, 0)
  • Teach students to:
    • Plot ordered pairs.
    • Identify quadrants.
    • See patterns in points (shapes, horizontal vs. vertical movement).
  • Use simple figures on the grid (rectangles, triangles) to connect geometry and graphing.
